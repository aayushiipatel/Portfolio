Personal Background

I was born in Valsad, India and I moved to the United States in 2015. I moved around a lot growing up; apart from India and the US, I lived in Saudi Arabia for a few years as well.

Moving to America after living most of my life in India was not easy. I still dwell on the dichotomy between American and Indian culture, with a respect for both. Over the years I've learned that it’s okay to feel like you’re neither here nor there as we are all shaped by the experiences we’ve lived through.

After being in the US for 9 years, I call New York my home. Growing up, visiting New York was the biggest dream and it was so surreal when I first moved here. I still have moments when I don't believe I'm actually in NEW YORK. I love being here but I still have days when I miss India, my family there, and the food!

Living in different countries has helped me grow as a person. It has taught me how to flow with situations, people and the world around me. I became more independent and open to new, exciting and sometimes terrifying challenges. And the most rewarding of all: the opportunity to learn and develop as a person, because it helped me discover things about myself that I never knew, my beliefs, my passions, my character. Through interacting with a foreign society, my eyes were opened to all sorts of aspects of life that would be difficult to learn in one country.
